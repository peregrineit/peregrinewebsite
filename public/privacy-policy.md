## 4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

### 4.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, making it easier to use the site and providing valuable analytics data.

### 4.2 Types of Cookies We Use

**Strictly Necessary Cookies:**
- Essential for Website functionality
- Enable basic features like page navigation and access to secure areas
- Cannot be disabled without affecting Website functionality

**Performance and Analytics Cookies:**
- Google Analytics (tracks user behavior and Website performance)
- Help us understand how visitors use the Website
- Provide statistics on page views, traffic sources, and user paths
- All data is aggregated and anonymous

**Functionality Cookies:**
- Remember your preferences and settings
- Enable enhanced features and personalization
- May remember your language preference or region

**Marketing and Targeting Cookies:**
- Track your browsing activity across websites
- Used to deliver relevant advertisements
- Measure advertising campaign effectiveness
- Provided by third-party advertising networks

**Third-Party Cookies:**

We use the following third-party services that may place cookies:
- **Google Analytics:** Website analytics and reporting
- **Google Ads / Facebook Pixel:** Advertising and retargeting
- **LinkedIn Insights:** Analytics and advertising
- **Hotjar / Microsoft Clarity:** User behavior analysis and session recording
- **HubSpot / Mailchimp:** Marketing automation and email services

### 4.3 Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through:
- **Browser Settings:** Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies
- **Cookie Consent Tool:** Use our cookie banner preferences (if available)
- **Opt-Out Links:** Visit Network Advertising Initiative (http://www.networkadvertising.org/choices/) or Digital Advertising Alliance (http://www.aboutads.info/choices/)

**Note:** Disabling cookies may affect Website functionality and your user experience.

### 4.4 Do Not Track Signals

Currently, we do not respond to "Do Not Track" signals from browsers. We will continue to monitor industry developments regarding DNT standards.
