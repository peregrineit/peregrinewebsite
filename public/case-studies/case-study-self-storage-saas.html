Overview

The Operator's Problem

A fast-growing self-storage operator managing 15 facilities across Spain and the UK was running their entire operation on spreadsheets, manual invoicing, and a patchwork of disconnected tools. Unit availability was tracked on whiteboards. Access codes were handed out on paper. Billing errors were costing them thousands every month.

They needed a centralized SaaS platform that could handle everything ‚Äî from a customer booking a unit online to automated monthly billing, smart access control, and real-time occupancy dashboards ‚Äî across all their locations from a single admin panel.

Key Takeaways

What Made This Work

Self-storage software often fails because it treats facility management as a simple CRUD problem. The real complexity is in the lifecycle automation ‚Äî what happens when a payment fails on day 15, the tenant's access needs to be restricted on day 20, a late fee needs to apply on day 30, and the unit needs to be flagged for lien processing on day 60. Getting that workflow engine right is what separates a prototype from a production SaaS.

The multi-tenant architecture was critical. Each facility operates with its own pricing rules, unit types, access schedules, and billing cycles ‚Äî but the owner sees everything rolled up into unified reporting. This balance between local autonomy and centralized visibility is what operators actually need.
