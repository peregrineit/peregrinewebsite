Overview

The Company's Problem

A mid-size logistics company running last-mile delivery operations across three Canadian provinces had no real-time visibility into their fleet. Dispatchers relied on phone calls to check driver locations. Route planning was done manually each morning. When a delivery failed, nobody knew until the driver returned to the depot hours later.

They needed a platform that could track every vehicle in real-time, automate dispatch and route optimization, give customers live delivery ETAs, and provide management with operational analytics ‚Äî all without requiring drivers to learn a complex system.

Key Takeaways

What Made This Work

The biggest technical challenge wasn't GPS tracking ‚Äî that's straightforward. It was handling 350+ vehicles sending location pings every 15 seconds while simultaneously recalculating ETAs for thousands of active deliveries. Redis Pub/Sub as the event bus was the right call ‚Äî it gave us the throughput for real-time updates without overwhelming the PostgreSQL database with write operations.

On the driver side, the app had to work in areas with poor cellular coverage. We built an offline queue that stores delivery completions and location data locally, then syncs when connectivity returns. This meant zero lost data even in rural delivery zones.
