Overview

The Healthcare Group's Challenge

A multi-specialty healthcare group operating 30+ clinics across two provinces was running on a 15-year-old legacy system that couldn't handle their growth. Appointment scheduling required phone calls. Patient records were siloed per location ‚Äî if a patient visited a different clinic, their history was invisible. Telehealth wasn't even possible on their existing infrastructure.

They needed a modern, HIPAA-compliant platform that unified patient records across all locations, enabled online booking and telehealth, automated billing and insurance claims, and gave clinic managers real-time operational dashboards ‚Äî without disrupting daily patient care during migration.

Key Takeaways

What Made This Work

Healthcare platforms fail when security is treated as an afterthought. We designed the entire data layer around HIPAA requirements from day one ‚Äî encryption at rest and in transit, complete audit logging, role-based access control, and session management. Retrofitting security into an existing architecture is ten times harder than building it in from the start.

The phased migration was critical. We couldn't take 30 clinics offline simultaneously. By rolling out in waves of 5, we caught integration edge cases at pilot clinics before they affected the wider network. Staff at each clinic got hands-on training during their transition week, which meant adoption was near-instant rather than a months-long struggle.
