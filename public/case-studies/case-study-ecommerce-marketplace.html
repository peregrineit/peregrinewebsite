Overview

The Founder's Vision

A startup founder in the Middle East wanted to build a curated multi-vendor marketplace connecting local artisans and small businesses with consumers across the region. The existing off-the-shelf solutions like Shopify or WooCommerce couldn't handle the multi-vendor complexity ‚Äî split payments, vendor-specific shipping rules, multi-currency support, and Arabic/English bilingual interfaces.

They needed a custom-built marketplace that could onboard vendors in minutes, handle per-vendor commission structures, manage split payments automatically, and scale from launch to thousands of orders per day without architecture changes.

Our Approach

The Architecture

We built the marketplace on a modular architecture where the storefront, vendor dashboard, admin panel, and payment engine operate as independent services communicating through a central API gateway. This separation meant we could scale the storefront independently during flash sales without touching the vendor management layer.

Key Takeaways

What Made This Work

The hardest part of a multi-vendor marketplace isn't the storefront ‚Äî it's the money flow. Split payments, vendor commissions, refund routing back to the right vendor, multi-currency conversion, and tax handling across jurisdictions create a complexity that most off-the-shelf solutions simply can't manage. Stripe Connect gave us the financial infrastructure, but the business logic layer on top ‚Äî commission tiers, payout schedules, dispute flows ‚Äî had to be custom-built.

Real-time inventory management was the second major challenge. When two customers add the last item to their cart simultaneously, only one should be able to check out. We implemented Redis-based stock locking at the cart level, not just at checkout, which eliminated overselling completely.
